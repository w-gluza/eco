History Of Plastic

At the beginning of the 20th century the first plastic, based on a synthetic polymer, was invented. The end of World war 1 brought about an explosion in the creation of new plastics and it became increasingly popular. By the 40s and 50s mass production had begun. As the single-use products industry continues to promote the convenience of a throw-away lifestyle, the vast flood of discarded plastic becomes an extremely noticeable and concerning form of pollution. At this point, plastic packaging has long overtaken all other kinds of containers.

According to the world economic forum, if we continue to produce plastic at predicted rates, plastic in the ocean will outweigh fish pound for pound by 2050. However, the world is becoming more and more aware of the situation and by working together we still have time to reverse the effects
